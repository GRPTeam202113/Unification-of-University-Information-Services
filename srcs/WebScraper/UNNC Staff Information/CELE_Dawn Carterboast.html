Biography

Dawn joined UNNC in 2013 and has over 17 years of teaching experience and 9 years’ experience teaching in higher education. She currently convenes the MA module, Advanced English Discourse Skills, works with the Writing Lab and teaches PY students. She is a Senior Fellow in the HEA and a passionate teacher with experience of supporting staff, students and international scholars at UNNC. Dawn focuses on developing effective learning environments and approaches to support and guide students, and enjoys adapting syllabi and creating and modifying materials in order to incorporate the needs of the students. For the last five years she has provided academic guidance to PG and UG students through academic advising sessions and workshops, and has also spent the last few summers advising on PG (including TESOL) dissertations and assignments. Dawn has a passion for the use of technology in the classroom and has provided professional development sessions for teachers in both the use of Smartboards and use of mirroring technology.

Another passion is travelling and experiencing different cultures and she has previously taught EAP, business English, English for examinations and General English in various countries around the world (The U.K., China, The Sultanate of Oman, Vietnam, South Korea and Tunisia). In her spare time, she indulges herself with her obsession of alternative therapies, herbal remedies, and essential oils.


Teaching

Advanced English Discourse Skills (PG), Academic Literacy Development Workshops (UG & PG) Reading and Writing in Academic Contexts (PY), Listening and Speaking in Academic Contexts (PY), Oral Communication Skills (PY), Independent Research Project (specifically for FHSS students (PY)), English for Specific Academic Contexts  (PY),  Listening, Discussion and Presentation Skills (PS), Reading, Writing and Research Skills (PS)


Research interests

Current research interests are in the areas of genre analysis and corpus linguistics concentrating on the text types and language that UNNC students need and can produce prior to them entering their specific fields of study.


Conference presentations

(2017) Institutional EAP Needs Analysis at an EMI University in China. Faces of English 2: Teaching and Researching Academic and Professional English, University of Hong Kong, 2nd June 2017
