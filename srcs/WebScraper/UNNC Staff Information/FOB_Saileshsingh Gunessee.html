Biography

Saileshsingh Gunessee has been with NUBS China since 2007. He completed his PhD in Economics at the School of Economics of the University of Nottingham. His research covers the areas of ‘Spatial Economics’, ‘Economics of International Business’, ‘Experimental & Behavioural Economics’ and ‘China-related Economics’. Some of his research work have led to a great affinity towards micro data and the use of microeconometrics to analyse such data. He teaches ‘Firm Strategy and Internationalisation’ at the undergraduate level. He has also taught Research Methods and Microeconomics at all levels.  


Teaching

Sailesh has taught mostly in the area of Microeconomics and Methods (i.e. Statistics and Econometrics) and has some Exec Ed Teaching experience. 

This academic year, he is teaching：

  • Firm Strategy and Internationalisation

Research interests

Sailesh has research interests in spatial economics, economics of international business, experimental & behavioural economics, and China-related economics. His recent research covers themes such as social preferences, natural disasters’ economic and supply chain impact, CSR in a global context, geography of venture capital and private equity financing, geography of innovation, and Chinese MNEs global expansion.

Sailesh is interested in supervising PhDs in any of these areas: spatial economics, behavioural economics, experimental economics, and international economics.
