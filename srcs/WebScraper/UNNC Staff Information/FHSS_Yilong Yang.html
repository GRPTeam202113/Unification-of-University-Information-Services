Biography

Yilong has lived and studied in China, the US, Japan, and the UK. During his time in the US, he worked with Japanese language students from a variety of linguistic backgrounds, including native speakers of English, Chinese, and Korean, and Japanese heritage learners. He also has experience teaching English as a second language.

 

After college, Yilong completed master’s degrees in applied linguistics and mind, brain, and education. His MSc dissertation used psycholinguistic methods to examine native Japanese speakers’ reading processing of conjugated kanji-kana-combined verbs. Currently, Yilong’s academic interests include language disorders, mental health in higher education, bilingualism in children on the autism spectrum, and non-linguistic cognitive consequences of bilingualism across the lifespan.
