Biography

I have been teaching EFL for the past 20 years in Japan, South Korea, China, Thailand, Myanmar, Vietnam, Saudi Arabia, Iraq, and Canada. I have taught in private, institutional, and public schools to children, teenagers, and adults. My teaching experience includes TOEFL, IELTS, ESP, EAP, and Teacher Training for children and adults. I have managed schools, demonstrated lessons to prospective students, edited and proofread dissertations, fellowship applications, and scientific reports.


Teaching

Undergraduate:

• RWAC (Reading and Writing in Academic Contexts)

• LSAC (Listening and Speaking in Academic Contexts)

• ESAC (English in Specific Academic Contexts)

• AOP (Academic Oral Presentations)

• IELTS Classes


Research interests

My main areas of research interest include discourse analysis, motivational orientations in EFL classrooms, communicative competence, and Dogme. I have conducted workshops and presented papers in classroom management skills, building rapport, and conflict management.

 


Conference presentations

October 25, 2014, American University of Iraq, Sulaimani/IATEFL Conference-'Classroom Management and Building Rapport in Secondary School Education,' for Iraqi Secondary School teachers in Kurdistan, Iraq.
