Biography

Dr. Anthony Bellotti is Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at University of Nottingham Ningbo, China. He received his PhD in machine learning from Royal Holloway, University of London in 2006 and was a Research Fellow in the Credit Research Centre at the University of Edinburgh from 2007 to 2010. He was senior lecturer at Imperial College London until 2019 where he taught quantitative methods in retail finance. His main research area is machine learning, with particular interest in credit risk models, dynamic survival models and reliable machine learning. He has published extensively on these topics in international refereed journals with 18 published papers over 10 years.

Research interests

Application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to Credit Risk Modelling

This is a topic of increasing interest to financial institutions. On the one hand, AI is an opportunity potentially providing more accurate risk management, whilst on the other, AI is a challenge to ensure they are deployed in a fair, trustworthy and transparent manner (explainable AI). At this time we have several interesting topics:-

  • Development of dynamic credit risk models using machine learning to provide Age-Period-Cohort profiles for customer segments that can be used as part of risk forecasting and stress testing.
  • Development of Early warning Systems (EWS) for corporate fraud using machine learning.
  • Document processing and management system with Knowledge Graphs.

For student entry in September 2022, we have two PhD project proposals. If you are interested, please email me.

PhD Project 1: New techniques for automated fraud detection

Explore supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques for automated fraud detection, with special attention to the problem of class imbalance, using generative methods to simulate fraud cases. Use reinforcement learning to construct optimal fraud detection strategies. Analysis of fraudulent behavior data on mobile devices.

PhD Project 2: Financial Data Management

Many financial documents are still stored on paper or in diverse electronic form. In this project, the student will work on a data management system for collecting, storing and organizing financial documents. This will involve automatic document detection and classification, to aid document analysis and noise reduction, and document integration. Technologies include machine learning and knowledge graphs.

 

Reliable Machine Learning through Conformal Prediction

Conformal predictors (CP) are machine learning algorithms that output prediction sets, rather than point predictions. They have the important property that they are marginally valid at any user-defined confidence level given minimal distributional assumptions; i.e. exchangeability. For example, if I set a 95% confidence level then the probability that the true outcome is in the prediction set will be guaranteed to be 95%.

In my research, I am interested in extending CP to have the additional goals:-
(1) Optimality, in the sense that predictive efficiency (i.e. size of prediction sets) is maximized;
(2) Approximation to conditional validity; i.e. validity should hold approximately for sub-segments of the population.

 

Artificial Intelligence Validation

Working with the Validate AI community (validateai.org), this research looks at topics of vulnerability for applied AI. In particular, I am interested in robustness of machine learning models across periods of crisis and extensive population drift.
