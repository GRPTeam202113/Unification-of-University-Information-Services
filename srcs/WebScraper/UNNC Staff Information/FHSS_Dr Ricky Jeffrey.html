Qualifications

Doctorate in Education, School of Education, University of Nottingham (doctoral thesis on the criterion of practicality in evaluating educational assessments, externally examined by Prof. Antony Kunnan).

Diploma (DELTA) and Certificate (CELTA) in English Language Teaching to Adults.

BA in Philosophy, University of Cambridge.


Biography

Before joining the School of Education and English in 2021 I worked in a range of roles across teaching, teacher training, management, and assessment. In assessment I was a designer and developer on the large academic English testing system of UNNC’s Preliminary Year Programme, being fortunate to win the British Council’s annual Innovation in Assessment Prize for work completed in this role. In management I was the first director of the English Language Center at Zhejiang University’s International Campus in Haining, in partnership with the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and the University of Edinburgh, a role in which I set up all departmental curricula and systems, and earlier in my career managed a series of private EFL summer schools for Studio Cambridge in the UK. For teacher training I’ve previously taught on two Masters courses for pre-service teachers of English, including the University of Edinburgh’s large MSc TESOL programme, and on various short teacher training courses for in-service teachers. Apart from this I’ve also taught a range of courses in English, education, and social science research methods at other universities in the UK and China, including the University of Warwick, Tsinghua University, and Northeast Normal University. Now here in the School of Education and English much of my work relates to the MA TESOL. I’m particularly enthusiastic about dissemination and discussion of research between researchers and practitioners; in addition to reviewing for several journals, I’m also an “ambassador” with the US-based non-profit Center for Open Science, as well as a member of the steering committee for EdArxiv, the largest open online repository of educational research, and enjoy using Twitter to discuss teaching and research. I currently speak Chinese at around intermediate or upper intermediate level, and welcome all opportunities for practice and further improvement….


Teaching

Courses across the MA TESOL programme, and supervision of EdD and PhD.

Module convenor for Language Testing and Assessment.


Research interests

Currently my main research interests are educational assessment (including language assessment), pedagogy (across content subjects as well as language), English for academic purposes, TESOL in the Chinese context, content and language integrated learning (CLIL), and online/digital education, as well as social science research methodology generally. For the past few years I’ve also had an interest in the online misinformation problem in the UK and similar countries, and how educational research might be able to help in addressing it.
