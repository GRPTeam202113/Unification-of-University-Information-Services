Biography

Dr Mengxia Xu received his PhD degree in Energy & Environmental Engineering from Zhejiang University in 2009. Then, he worked as Engineer and later promoted to Senior Engineer in Ningbo Environmental Monitoring Center. After that, he obtained the postdoctoral experience in the United States both in the Department of Chemistry and Environmental Sciences at Louisiana State University. Dr Xu returned to his hometown and joined the University of Nottingham Ningbo China (UNNC) in 2016 as the Assistant Professor in Environmental Engineering. Since joining UNNC, he has been teaching three different levels of separation processes related core Chemical Engineering and Environmental Engineering courses.

 

Dr Xu’s research experiences primarily center on the toxic by-products from thermal conversion processes, e.g., persistent organic pollutants (POPs), environmentally persistent free radicals (EPFRs) as well as conventional pollutants such as Hg0, SO2, NOx, CO2 and VOCs. Meanwhile, he has conducted fundamental research in the reaction mechanisms and kinetics of pyrolysis of lignin model compounds. So far, he has published over 20 SCI papers, mostly in the CAS Q1/Q2 journals (e.g., Environmental Science & Technology, Journal of Hazardous Materials, Energy Conversion and Management, Energy, Fuel, Chemosphere, Journal of Analytical and Applied Pyrolysis and Applied Surface Science) with a total Google Scholar citation over 400 and an h-index of 10. In addition, Dr Xu holds two invention patents and 1 utility model patent. As the PI/Co-I, Dr Xu has been cumulatively awarded nearly RMB 7 million grants, including the National Natural Science Foundation of China, Zhejiang Provincial Key R&D Program, Ningbo S&T Innovation 2025 Major Program and Ningbo Natural Science Foundation.

Research interests

  • Development of novel catalysts for pollution control (e.g., Hg0, VOCs and wastewater)
  • Electrocatalytic conversion of CO2 to C2 products
  • Microwave-assisted thermal conversion of organic solid wastes (e.g., sludge and plastics)
  • Harmless treatment and resource utilization of industrial/hazardous solid wastes
