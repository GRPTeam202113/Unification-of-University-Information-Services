Biography

I just returned to the University of Nottingham Ningbo this year, October 2020. Worked here before during the period from February 2013 to February 2015 as a Science Tutor.

I taught general chemistry, physical chemistry and advanced topics in physical chemistry for many years to both undergraduate and postgraduate students in different countries and Universities.

Over the last 13 years, I have been involved in delivering Science modules (Chemistry in addition to Physics, Math’s and Scientific Methods) to a Year one Foundation students. I have taught these modules to Foundation students in Kaplan International Colleges in Sheffield and London (2006 to 2012), as well as I taught these modules when I worked as part time tutor for ISC Kingston University and Bell Cambridge.

I also worked as postdoctoral fellow for Kyungpook National University in Taegu and Research Scientist and Lecturer in Kyunghee University in Seoul, where I did research in Photochemistry and taught advanced topics for postgraduate students, and I co-supervised M.Sc and Ph.D student’s research projects.

Scientific Methods


Research interests

I started my carrier in science as a spectroscopist in Tajura Nuclear Research Center and Laser Research Centre Tripoli-Libya, where I studied the light absorbance and luminescence properties of many small medicinal and organic molecules, then I did some work on radiation chemistry of amino acids and simple peptides, using different sources of radiations including gamma and neutrons.

Later on, in my Ph.D. project and after, my research interest focused on studying the reaction kinetics and reaction mechanisms of short lived transient species and free radicals that produced in laser photolysis of chemical compounds.
