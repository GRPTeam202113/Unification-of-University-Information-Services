Biography

Kian is from Malaysia; he completed his undergraduate at the University of Nottingham Malaysia; and his Masters and PhD in Economics at the Nottingham School of Economics, UK. He then spent a year as a research fellow at the Department of Economics, University of Melbourne and worked for two years as an Assistant Professor at Nottingham Malaysia before joining NUBS China in 2019.


Teaching

Kian teaches applied statistics (Quantitative Methods 1b) and numerical analysis (Stochastic Calculus and Numerical Analysis). He also convenes and delivers topics on applied econometrics to PhD students (Topics in Advanced Econometrics).


Research interests

Kian’s primary area of research is applied macroeconomics, with expertise in fiscal policy and tools from applied econometrics. His research centres on the effects of government spending on the U.S. economy, the effects of centralized fiscal transfers on the Chinese economy as well as growth and convergence in China. Kian is keen to supervise PhD candidates who have an interest in these topics.

Current research topics include:

  • Measuring The Fiscal Multiplier When Plans Take Time to Implement (with K. Lee, J.C. Morley and K. Shields), CAMA Working Paper 13/2019.
  • Growth versus Equity: The Effects of Centralized Fiscal Transfers on Provincial Economies in China (with K. Matthews and B. Wang)
  • Converging to Shanghai: Evidence from Chinese Provinces across Four Decades of Growth (with K. Matthews and B. Wang)
  • Net Foreign Assets Dynamics: The Persistence and Sources of Shocks to Net Foreign Assets in 12 EU Countries. MPRA
  • The Effects of Mediums of Instruction on Educational- and Labor Market Outcomes: Evidence from Malaysia (with R. Parinduri) MPRA Paper No. 87560.
