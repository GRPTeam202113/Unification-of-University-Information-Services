Biography

Dr Sathyaprakash Balaji Makam (M S Balaji) is an Associate Professor in Marketing at Nottingham University Business School China (NUBS). Prior to joining NUBS on September 2015, he held a Lecturer (research) position at Taylor’s Business School, Taylor’s University, Malaysia, and Assistant Professor position at IBS Hyderabad, IFHE University, India. He was a visiting research scholar at Whitman School of Management, Syracuse University, USA between August 2007 and July 2008. Prior to the academic career, he worked in the Healthcare Industry for more than 3 years in India. Dr. Balaji has published his research in leading marketing and business management journals including Journal of Retailing, Journal of Travel Research, Journal of Business Research, European Journal of Marketing, Information and Management, International Journal of Hospitality Management, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, Journal of Services Marketing, Service Industries Journal and others. He serves as a reviewer for many leading marketing and business management journals.

Research interests

Dr Sathyaprakash Balaji Makam’s research interest in three main areas. In the first area, he is interested in understanding the nature of customer-service provider interaction between both human-human and human-nonhuman actors. Specifically, he focuses on understand the positive and negative interactions between the customer and the service provider from multiple actors’ perspectives. Research work in this area is published in journals including Journal of Business Research, European Journal of Marketing, Technological Forecasting and Social Change, Information & Management, Journal of Marketing Management, Journal of Services Marketing, Information Technology & People, and others. The second area focuses on sustainability communication and sustainable consumption. Specifically, I am interested in understanding the effectiveness of sustainability communication on social media platforms. The research in this area is published in Journal of Travel Research, International Journal of Contemporary Hospitality Management, Journal of Business Research, and others. The third area focuses on the role of haptic attributes and haptic evaluation of products on consumption behavior. Research work in this area is published in Journals such as Journal of Retailing, Asia Pacific Journal of Logistics and Marketing, and others.
