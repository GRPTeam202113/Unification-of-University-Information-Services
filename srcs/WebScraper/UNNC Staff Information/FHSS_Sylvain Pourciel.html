Biography

I was born and raised in the south of France where I did all my studies. My original major is English language and literature.

After graduating from a professional master’s degree in Teaching French as a Second Language and a research master’s degree in Applied Linguistics, I spent almost three years in Toulouse (my hometown) teaching French at various levels and for different purposes at the University of Toulouse and at the Alliance Française. I also co-organized Summer Schools taking place in Toulouse for engineering students of the University of Texas in Austin.

In 2015, I was hired by Sun Yat Sen University in Guangdong province (China) where I spent three years and a half before joining UNNC.

My interests in life include, but are not limited to, taking photos, coffee, playing music, learning more about words in any languages, animals and animal rights, independent cinema.

Research interests

Research Interest

Applied Linguistics

My main research interest is vocabulary, both from pedagogical and psycholinguistic perspectives, and in particular :

  • Vocabulary acquisition
  • Formulaic language processing
  • Formulaic language acquisition

 

Gender Studies

I am part of the Gender Studies Research Group and in this field my interests are:

  • Sociology of queer groups
  • New Queer Art, Cinema and Media
