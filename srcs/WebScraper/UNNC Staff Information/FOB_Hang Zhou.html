Biography

Dr Hang Zhou is an assistant professor in Finance at Nottingham University Business School China. Prior to Joining NUBS in October 2020, he was at University of Edinburgh (Assistant Professor in Finance).

Hang’s research is mainly in the areas of Capital Market Asset Pricing and Information Efficiency. His work has been of interests to both practitioners and policy makers, leading to research engagement with financial institutions such as Shanghai Stock Exchange, London Stock exchange and Aberdeen Standard Investment.

Hang has made contribution to international journals such as Journal of Financial Markets and European Journal of Finance. His work has also been accepted for presentation by international conferences such as FMA-Asia/Pacific conference and MIT Asia conference in Accounting.

Hang serves as a referee for academic journals such as International Review of Financial Analysis, European Journal of Finance, Economic Modelling and Finance Research Letters.

Research interests

Dr Hang Zhou’s research covers a wide range of topics in financial markets including information dissemination, asset pricing, market microstructure and textual analysis.
