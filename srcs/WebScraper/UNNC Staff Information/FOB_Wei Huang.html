Biography

Wei Huang is an associate professor in finance and accounting and head of department of finance, accounting and economics at Nottingham University Business School China (Faculty of Business). Prior to joining Nottingham Ningbo in 2007 as assistant professor, he worked in the University of Bristol as teaching fellow (2003 – 2006) and Northumbria University as senior lecturer (2006 – 2007). Wei completed his PHD in finance in the University of Bristol while working as a full-time faculty member. He is also a holder of the Financial Risk Manager (FRM®) and CPA (Australia) designations, and has extensive experience in consultancy and executive education. He also serves on corporate boards as an independent director. Wei has been consistently ranked among the best teachers in Nottingham University Business School China according to the Student Evaluations of Teaching (SET) surveys. The department of finance, accounting and economics is the largest department/school within the University of Nottingham Ningbo China with 3 sections and around 50 faculty members.

Wei is currently an associate editor of International Review of Financial Analysis (ABDC-A/ABS3/SSCI), International Review of Economics and Finance (ABDC-A/ABS2/SSCI), Finance Research Letters (ABDC-A/ABS2/SSCI) and the China area editor of Research in International Business and Finance (ABDC-B/ABS2/SSCI). His research lies in the areas of corporate finance, financial accounting, and corporate innovation. He has a particular interest in the Chinese markets and firms and has supervised a number of PHD students to completion. To date, Wei has published 20 papers, including several single-authored ones, in ABS-3/ABDC-A ranked journals.
